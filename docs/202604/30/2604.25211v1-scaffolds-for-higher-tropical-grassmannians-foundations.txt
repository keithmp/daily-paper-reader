Scaffolds for Higher Tropical Grassmannians: Foundations 

NICK EARLY AND THOMAS LAM 

Abstract. Scaffolds are the one-dimensional skeleta of high-dimensional flag simplicial complexes of nonpositive curvature. They generalize the phylogenetic trees of Trop Gr(2 , n ) to arbitrary 

k, drawing together SL( k)-web bases, affine buildings, the combinatorics of the positive tropical Grassmannian and low-dimensional topology. We prove that scaffolds model points in all tropical Grassmannians via a k-point distance function. In this paper, we study in detail CAT(0) planar graphs, which are positive scaffolds for the tropical Grassmannian of three-planes. CAT(0) planar graphs are directed versions of the diskoids of Fontaine-Kamnitzer-Kuperberg, planar dual to SL(3)-webs. Our main result is the construction of a unique representation of any given integer positive tropical Plucker vector by a normal CAT(0) planar graph. We show that any normal CAT(0) planar graph embeds into the tropical linear space as a Lam-Postnikov membrane, and embeds into the Keel-Tevelev membrane within the affine building. We show that Early’s planar basis expansion can be computed directly from the strand combinatorics of the dual web, and connect this expansion to Petersen-Pylyavskyy-Speyer’s noncrossing tableaux, explored further in our companion paper.

In 2004, Speyer and Sturmfels [SS] introduced the tropical Grassmannian, a k(n − k) + 1 dimen-sional polyhedral fan in R(nk). Just as the Grassmannian is ubiquitous in mathematics, the tropical Grassmannian has emerged as a fundamental object in tropical geometry, and more broadly in com-binatorial algebraic geometry with applications to mathematical biology and theoretical physics. The tropical Grassmannian Trop Gr(2 , n ) of two-planes can be identified with the moduli space of phylogenetic trees, and plays an important role in mathematical biology. This moduli space can also be viewed as the tropicalization Trop M0,n of the moduli space M0,n of n-pointed rational curves. Whereas the combinatorial study of the tropical moduli spaces Trop Mg,n of higher genus curves has flourished in the last decade, the study of higher tropical Grassmannians Trop Gr( k, n )in recent years has been relatively subdued, especially when it comes to explicit combinatorial models; see [HJJS, CGUZ, FL]. The aim of this work is to propose a new combinatorial model for higher tropical Grassmannians: scaffolds , built from the bones of tropical linear spaces. Scaffolds are pairs ( Q, z) consisting of a directed graph Q equipped with n distinguished vertices 

z = ( z1, . . . , z n), satisfying conditions spelled out in Definitions 3.10 and 3.12. This distance function appears in the work of Le–O’Dorney, Le, and Fraser–Le [LO, Le21, FL] on higher laminations in affine buildings. The name scaffold reflects multiple aspects of the story. First, we expect a scaffold to be the one-dimensional skeleton of a ( k − 1)-dimensional simplicial complex of locally nonpositive curvature. Second, as we discuss below, tropical Pl¨ ucker vectors, matroid decompositions, tropical linear spaces can all be reconstructed from a scaffold. Finally, scaffolds often embed inside the buildings 

of Bruhat–Tits. In the case k = 2, the graphs ( Q, z) are exactly the metric or phylogenetic trees, where the distinguished vertices z are the leaves of the tree. The main result of this paper is a complete com-binatorial classification of positive scaffolds for the positive tropical Grassmannian Trop >0Gr(3 , n )of three-planes. The positive tropical Grassmannian Trop >0Gr( k, n ) ⊂ Trop Gr( k, n ) is a subfan introduced by Speyer and Williams [SW05], motivated by total positivity. For k = 3, positive scaffolds take a concrete combinatorial form: they are the normal CAT(0) planar graphs (Q, z) (Definition 4.6). We summarize our main theorem as follows. Theorem 1.1 (Theorem 4.8) . Every integer point in Trop >0Gr(3 , n ), modulo lineality, is modeled by a unique cyclic-less normal CAT(0) planar graph (Q, z).

CAT(0) planar graphs are the dual quivers (cf. Postnikov [Pos] and Shen–Sun–Weng [SSW25]) to the non-elliptic SL(3)-webs in the sense of Kuperberg. They are an orientation of the one-skeleton of the diskoids of Fontaine–Kamnitzer–Kuperberg [FKK]. We demonstrate the utility of scaffolds through several structural results: (1) We show that the normal CAT(0) planar graphs of Theorem 1.1 embed into the tropical linear spaces of Speyer [Spe] as a membrane in the sense of Lam and Postnikov [LP20]. This allows us to relate (Theorem 6.10) the membranes of Lam and Postnikov to the membranes of Keel and Tevelev [KT]. (2) Associated to a point π• ∈ Trop Gr( k, n ) is a matroid decomposition of the hypersimplex ∆( k, n ). We show (Section 6) that these matroids are recovered from the combinatorics of distance minimizers in ( Q, z). (3) We give an explicit combinatorial formula (Theorem 5.8) for the expansion of π•(Q, z) in terms of the planar basis of Early [Ear22, Ear24]. This solves an open problem concerning the construction of Feynman diagrams for the P2 case of generalized biadjoint scalar am-plitudes m(3)

(4) As another application, we prove (Proposition 7.1) the conjectured recursion for the (trop-ical) symbol alphabet of Ren–Spradlin–Volovich [RSV] in the case k = 3. Our results here rely on a duality theorem from our companion paper [EL26+]. A normal CAT(0) planar graph representing a point in Trop >0 Gr(3 , 12) is illustrated in Fig. 1. We remark that our proof of the main theorem does not invoke the machinery of affine buildings. The Dressian Dr( k, n ) is the space of all tropical Pl¨ ucker vectors, also known as valuated matroids (see Section 2.2). (A) We expect that describing explicit scaffolds for Trop >0Gr( k, n ) is equivalent to the combinatorial-representation-theoretic problem of constructing explicit SL(k)-web bases. In future work, we plan to describe explicitly scaffolds for Trop >0Gr(4 , n ), noting the recently discovered hourglass plabic graph SL(4)-web basis [GPPSS]. The case k = 4 is of particular interest because of its applications to scattering amplitudes in physics [ALS21b, DFGK, HP]. The positive tropical Grassmannians Trop >0Gr(4 , n ) have been conjectured to control the symbol of N = 4 super Yang-Mills amplitudes. We expect that in general scaffolds for Trop >0Gr( k, n ) are one-skeleta of ( k − 1)-dimensional complexes satisfying a local curvature condition. Explicit descriptions of these scaffolds could yield an SL( k)-web basis for k ≥ 5. (B) Whereas Trop >0Gr(2 , n ) is the moduli space of planar phylogenetic trees, the full tropical Grassmannian Trop Gr(2 , n ) is the moduli space of all phylogenetic trees. We establish an anal-ogous (but partial) result for k = 3: we show in Theorem 4.8 that every labeled CAT(0) planar graph ( ⃗Q, z) gives a point in Dr(3 , n ), where the labeled vertices z can now be placed anywhere in the graph - not necessarily in cyclic order, and not necessarily on the boundary. We show in Theorem 3.14 that every integer point in Trop Gr( k, n ) has a weak scaffold, up to lineality. This is based on the connection between the tropical Grassmannian Trop Gr( k, n )and the affine building of PGL( k), and in particular on the results of Le [Le21]. Henceforth we work with k = 3, and n ≥ k.4.1. Main definition. We introduce the main object of this work. 

Definition 4.1. A CAT(0) planar graph Q is a nonempty connected directed planar graph with all interior faces being oriented triangles, such that 

(1) All interior vertices have degree at least 6, and 

(2) All edges and vertices are on the perimeter of some face. 

CAT(0) planar graphs are obtained from the one-skeleta of diskoids [FKK] by equipping the edges with directions; see Part 5. The boundary ∂Q , when traversed counterclockwise is a closed curve in the plane. A vertex 

v ∈ V (Q) is called a cut vertex if it is a self-intersection point of this curve. An edge e ∈ E(Q) is called a cut edge if it is traversed twice by the curve, necessarily once in each direction. Often we will restrict our attention to CAT(0) planar graphs without cut edges. However, when cut edges do exist, we use the following convention. 

Convention 4.2. If v → u is a cut edge of Q, then δ(v, u ) = 1 and δ(u, v ) = 2 . In other words, when computing directed distance we can use the cut edge backwards where it has distance 2. 

Thus, in all cases, the directed distance δ(v, u ) is the minimum directed distance of a path from v to u where traversing an edge according to the orientation (resp. against the orientation) contributes 1 (resp. 2), agreeing with (3.1). Since a CAT(0) planar graph Q is assumed to be connected, it has a finite-valued directed distance function δ(·, ·). For vertices v, w ∈ V (Q), the combinatorial distance ℓ(v, w ) is the minimal edge count in any path, ignoring directions of edges. Scaffolds for Higher Tropical Grassmannians: Foundations 13

6. Membranes: LP vs. KT 

In this section, we discuss membranes in the sense of Lam-Postnikov (LP) [LP20] and in the sense of Keel-Tevelev (KT) [KT], respectively. Roughly speaking, KT membranes are naturally identified with triangulated tropical linear spaces, while LP membranes are certain two-dimensional surfaces in the bounded complex of a positive tropical linear space. The map μ in (3.3) sends a normal CAT(0) planar graph, or more generally a canvas in a scaffold (Remark 3.15), to a LP membrane. The Keel-Tevelev isomorphism (Theorem 21.1) then maps the LP membrane into a KT membrane within the affine building B of PGL( k). In this section, we let W be a normal web and ( Q, z) be the dual normal CAT(0) planar graph. In particular, the boundary vertices z are arranged in counterclockwise cyclic order on the boundary 

∂Q , and we always assume that n ≥ 3. 6.1. Lam-Postnikov membranes. In [LP20], membranes are defined in the general setting of plabic graphs. We specialize to non-elliptic webs and change coordinates to our conventions. 

Definition 6.1. Let W be a web where boundary vertices have degree one and (Q, z) its dual labeled CAT(0) planar graph. A membrane M(W ) for W is a lifting of each vertex v ∈ V (Q) to an integer point μ(v) ∈ Zn/1 satisfying the following property: if v → w is an edge of Q and e
