> Abstract. In the present paper, we derive Berry-Esseen bounds for the estimation of diversity indices on countable alphabets. A general non-asymptotic convergence rate is established for the plug-in estimator of a wide class of indices, including Simp-son‚Äôs index and Re¬ī nyi‚Äôs entropy. For the practically crucial case of Shannon entropy, we provide explicit Berry-Esseen bounds for the standard plug-in estimator, as well as for two widely used bias-corrected variants, the Miller-Madow and the jackknife estimators.

1. Introduction 

Diversity indices provide the fundamental framework for quantifying the uncertainty or information content of a probability distribution over an alphabet. In this setting, the absence of natural ordering renders classical moments like variance and standard deviation undefined. Beyond information theory, these indices are widely applied in ecology, genetics, and linguistics, where they quantify the complexity of populations such as species assemblages, cancer cell types, and author vocabularies. The earliest contributions to this field include Shannon‚Äôs entropy [12] and Simpson‚Äôs index [13], both of which remain widely used. A unifying framework for these indices was established by R¬ī enyi [11], who introduced a parametric family of entropies that includes both Shannon‚Äôs entropy and Simpson‚Äôs index as special cases. This framework was further advanced by Hill [6], who showed that a broad class of diversity indices are equivalent under monotonic transformations. Zhang and Grabchak [16] showed that a large class of diversity indices in the literature can be represented as linear combinations of an entropic basis and proposed corresponding nonparametric estimators. The most prevalent approach for evaluating a diversity index is the plug-in estimator. While extensive research has addressed its asymptotic properties for finite alphabets [16], the case of countably infinite alphabets presents additional challenges. In previous papers, Grabchak and Zhang [5] studied the asymptotic distribution of the plug-in estimator for a large class of diversity indices. Additionally, Paninski [8] and Zhang and Zhang [17] proved the asymptotic normality of the plug-in estimator of Shannon‚Äôs entropy, and Zhang and Shi [15] extended this analysis to generalized Shannon entropy.

To further reduce the sample bias, a number of bias-corrected modifications have been proposed in Pinchas et al. [10]. Among these, two of the most popular are the Miller-Madow estimator [7], which applies an additive correction based on the number of observed symbols, and the jackknife estimator [14], a resampling-based technique that systematically reduces bias by recomputing the estimate on subsamples of the data. Chen et al. [2] provided sufficient conditions for the asymptotic normality of these two bias-corrected estimators. However, these studies primarily establish asymptotic distributions without providing explicit rates of convergence. In the present paper, we establish Berry-Esseen bounds for a large class of diversity indices and their bias-corrected estimators, thereby offering explicit non-asymptotic convergence rates. Section 2 states the main results. Our results hold for a wide family of diversity indices including Simpson‚Äôs index and R¬ī enyi‚Äôs entropy. While Shannon‚Äôs entropy [12] does not directly fall into our framework, we provide a separate proof for this specific case. The result is then extended to the Miller-Madow and jackknife estimators of Shannon‚Äôs entropy. In Section 3, we discuss some examples to show that our conditions are readily satisfied. The proofs of all results are detailed in Section 4. 2. Diversity Indices
